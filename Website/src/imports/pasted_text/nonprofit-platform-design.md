Design a clean, modern, accessible web platform for small and mid-sized nonprofits in British Columbia that helps them turn public goodwill into real support. The platform should feel warm, trustworthy, community-centered, and practical, not overly corporate or enterprise. The main users are nonprofit coordinators, volunteer managers, and small teams with limited technical experience, limited time, and sometimes older equipment. The design should prioritize simplicity, clarity, accessibility, and emotional trust.
The platform’s purpose is to help nonprofits:
launch outreach or fundraising campaigns
get matched with volunteers, student groups, or vetted outreach firms
manage campaign needs in one place
set up a simple payment portal for one-time and monthly donations
track support generated from each campaign
reduce the burden of manually coordinating people and outreach
The core message of the platform is:
Many nonprofits already have public goodwill around them, but they lack the infrastructure to convert that goodwill into donations, donor relationships, and sustained support.
Create a product that feels like a support hub for nonprofits, not just a marketplace. It should feel mission-driven, human, and approachable.
Brand and visual direction
Use a warm, optimistic, community-oriented visual style. Avoid sleek fintech or hard enterprise aesthetics. Make it feel like a nonprofit capacity-building tool that understands real community work. Use soft but professional colors such as deep green, muted blue, warm cream, off-white, soft teal, and gentle orange accents. Include subtle illustrations or icons related to community, people, outreach, support, donations, and connection. Prioritize readability and emotional warmth.
Design principles
simple and low-friction
very easy to understand for non-technical users
accessible and multilingual-friendly
mobile-responsive
high contrast and clear typography
clear buttons and straightforward flows
supportive language, not jargon-heavy
emphasize trust and transparency
make important information scannable
Target user
The main user is a nonprofit staff member who may be overwhelmed, under-resourced, and managing many responsibilities at once. They may need to launch a campaign quickly without building complex infrastructure. Design for someone who wants things to “just work.”
Core product concept
The platform helps nonprofits create a campaign by entering:
campaign goal
support needed
budget
location
cause area
timeline
whether they need volunteers, a professional outreach firm, or both
Then the platform helps them:
get matched with support
generate a campaign page
create a donation portal
enable monthly giving
view campaign results in a simple dashboard
Create the following screens
1. Landing page
A welcoming homepage for nonprofits.
Include:
hero section with headline and subheadline
CTA like “Start a Campaign” and “See How It Works”
visual showing nonprofit coordinator using the platform
section explaining the problem in a human way
section showing how the platform works in 3 simple steps
section explaining why this is better for small nonprofits
testimonials from nonprofit coordinators
section emphasizing accessibility, trust, and ease of use
footer with nonprofit-friendly navigation
Suggested hero message:
Turn community support into sustained impact
Subtext:
Launch outreach campaigns, connect with volunteers or trusted partners, and start accepting one-time and monthly donations, all in one place.
2. Nonprofit onboarding flow
A simple multi-step setup experience for nonprofits.
Steps should include:
organization name and cause
campaign type
fundraising goal
timeline
location
support type needed: volunteers, outreach firm, or hybrid
budget range
languages needed
vulnerable population checkbox to trigger trust/safety options
whether background checks are required
Keep the flow minimal, calm, and easy.
3. Campaign builder
A dashboard page where a nonprofit creates a new campaign.
Include:
campaign title
short description
fundraising target
preferred support type
target audience
outreach type
date range
donation page customization
monthly donation option toggle
campaign preview
Make it feel easy, guided, and not overwhelming.
4. Matching results page
Show matched support options for the nonprofit.
Include separate cards for:
volunteers
student groups
vetted outreach firms
hybrid recommendations
Each card should show:
support type
availability
languages
background check status
expected fit
estimated cost or free support
why it was recommended
The design should make the platform feel intelligent but still simple and human.
5. Donation portal setup page
This is very important.
Design a simple donation setup flow where nonprofits can create:
one-time donations
monthly donations
suggested donation tiers
campaign messaging
QR code and sharable link
donor thank-you message
donor contact capture
Make this page feel very easy and unintimidating, since smaller nonprofits may struggle with payment setup elsewhere.
6. Active campaign dashboard
Create a nonprofit-side dashboard showing:
money raised
monthly donors acquired
volunteer hours contributed
outreach activities completed
number of supporters engaged
campaign progress toward goal
recent activity feed
next actions
Use clean cards, simple graphs, and very easy navigation.
7. Trust and safety screen
A page that helps nonprofits manage sensitive settings.
Include:
background check requirements
role restrictions
vulnerable population indicator
approved messaging templates
partner vetting status
consent and privacy reminders
This should feel reassuring, not scary.
8. Mobile responsive version
Show what the key pages would look like on mobile, especially:
campaign setup
donation page
dashboard
supporter signup
Make sure it still feels clear and easy to use.
Functional ideas to visually represent
match nonprofits with the right type of help
convert outreach into recurring support
simplify campaign and donation setup
support nonprofits with limited capacity
help nonprofits retain supporters over time
UX tone
Use supportive nonprofit-friendly language such as:
“Get the support you need”
“Launch your campaign in minutes”
“Find the right help for your cause”
“Turn outreach into ongoing support”
“Set up monthly giving without the hassle”
Avoid language that sounds too salesy, too startup-like, or too enterprise-heavy.
Components to include
accessible navigation bar
clear CTA buttons
progress indicators
campaign cards
support match cards
metrics dashboard cards
donation tier selector
monthly giving toggle
empty state illustrations
trust badges
multilingual support indicator
Overall design goal
The platform should look like a mission-driven nonprofit support system that helps small organizations do more with less. It should feel empowering, calm, and trustworthy. The design should communicate that this platform understands the realities of community organizations and helps them turn goodwill into meaningful, lasting support.
